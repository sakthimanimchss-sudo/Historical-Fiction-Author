About the Series

Set against the backdrop of Viking invasions and the birth of England, The Saxon Chronicles follows the journey of a young Saxon nobleman caught between loyalty to his people and an unexpected destiny.

The year is 865 AD. The Great Heathen Army has landed on English shores, and the kingdoms of Northumbria, East Anglia, and Mercia are falling one by one. Only Wessex stands against the Viking tide. In these turbulent times, a young thegn named Eadric finds himself thrust into a world of shifting alliances, bloody battles, and impossible choices.

Over the course of four novels, we follow Eadric from a reluctant warrior to a legendary figure whose actions will help shape the future of England. The series weaves together historical figures like Alfred the Great and Guthrum the Viking with fictional characters, creating a rich tapestry of life in the Dark Ages.

The Real History

The Saxon Chronicles is rooted in meticulous research of 9th century England.

The series draws on the Anglo-Saxon Chronicle, Asser's Life of King Alfred, and archaeological evidence from Viking-age settlements. Key historical figures include:

  • Alfred the Great: King of Wessex who defended England against Viking conquest
  • Guthrum: Viking leader who fought against and was later baptized by Alfred
  • Ubba Ragnarsson: Legendary Viking chieftain and son of Ragnar Lothbrok
  • Æthelred: Alfred's brother and predecessor as King of Wessex
