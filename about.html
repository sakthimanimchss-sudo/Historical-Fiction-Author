Eleanor at the Bodleian Library, Oxford

The Woman Behind the Stories

Eleanor Hartwell is an award-winning historical fiction author whose work has been praised for its meticulous research, vivid storytelling, and emotional depth.

Born in York, England, Eleanor grew up surrounded by history—from the medieval city walls to the majestic York Minster. Her childhood was filled with stories of Vikings, kings, and common people whose lives shaped the world we know today. This early immersion in history sparked a lifelong passion that would eventually become her career.

After earning a degree in History from the University of Oxford and a Master's in Creative Writing from the University of East Anglia, Eleanor spent several years working as a historical researcher and editor before publishing her debut novel, The Last Kingdom, in 2015. The book's success launched The Saxon Chronicles series and established her as a distinctive voice in historical fiction.

Today, Eleanor divides her time between writing, research, and connecting with readers. She is a Fellow of the Royal Historical Society and serves on the advisory board of the Historical Writers' Association.

Writing Philosophy

"History is not just a record of what happened—it's a window into the human soul across time."

My approach to writing historical fiction begins with deep research. I believe that authenticity matters—not just in dates and events, but in the small details: how people spoke, what they ate, how they loved and grieved. I spend months, sometimes years, immersed in primary sources, visiting locations, and consulting with historians before writing a single word.

But research is only the foundation. At its heart, historical fiction is about people—their hopes, fears, and struggles. My goal is to make readers feel as though they're living alongside my characters, experiencing their world as vividly as if it were their own.

Beyond Writing

When I'm not researching or writing, you might find me leading historical walking tours in York, giving talks at literary festivals, or curled up with a stack of history books. I'm also an avid supporter of historical preservation causes and donate a portion of my royalties to organizations working to protect heritage sites.

I live in Oxfordshire with my family and two very demanding cats who believe they are the true authors of my books.
