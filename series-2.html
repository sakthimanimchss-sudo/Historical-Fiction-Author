The Tudor Intrigue

Love, betrayal, and power in the court of Henry VIII

3 Books 16th Century England 4.7 (1,800+ reviews)

About the Series

Step into the dangerous world of Tudor politics, where love and betrayal walk hand in hand.

The year is 1525. Henry VIII rules England with an iron fist, his desperate quest for a male heir threatening to tear the realm apart. Into this volatile court comes young Katherine Willoughby, a lady-in-waiting with a sharp mind and a dangerous secret—she was raised in the household of Thomas More and carries the scent of reform.

Over three gripping novels, we follow Katherine's journey from naive courtier to cunning survivor as she navigates the shifting sands of Tudor politics. She crosses paths with Anne Boleyn, Thomas Cromwell, and the king himself, learning that in the Tudor court, one wrong word can mean the difference between favor and the block.

The series weaves together meticulous historical research with compelling fiction, bringing to life the sights, sounds, and dangers of Tudor England.

The Real History

The Tudor Intrigue is grounded in extensive research of 16th century England and the Tudor court.

The series draws on primary sources including state papers, contemporary chronicles, and letters from the period. Key historical figures include:

  • Henry VIII: The mercurial king whose quest for a male heir changed England forever
  • Anne Boleyn: The second queen, whose rise and fall captivated Europe
  • Thomas Cromwell: The brilliant but ruthless architect of the Reformation
  • Thomas More: Scholar, statesman, and martyr who refused to bend to the king's will
  • Jane Seymour: The quiet queen who finally gave Henry his son
