About the Series

At the edge of the empire, a Roman legion faces its greatest challenge—and its greatest glory.

The year is 117 AD. Hadrian has just become emperor, and the northern frontier of Britannia is in chaos. The Ninth Legion has vanished, and rumors of rebellion sweep through the province. Into this turmoil steps Lucius Valerius Aquila, a young standard-bearer of the Twentieth Legion, tasked with discovering what happened to his comrades and restoring Roman honor.

But the truth he uncovers is far more dangerous than he imagined—a conspiracy that reaches the highest levels of power, and a enemy that fights not with swords but with secrets. From the walled cities of Roman Britain to the wild lands beyond Hadrian's future wall, Lucius must choose between duty to Rome and loyalty to the men who follow him.

This two-book series combines gripping military action with political intrigue, bringing the Roman occupation of Britain to vivid life through meticulous research and compelling characters.

The Real History

The Roman Eagle is grounded in the fascinating and mysterious history of Roman Britain.

The series draws on archaeological evidence, Roman military records, and the writings of Tacitus and Cassius Dio. Key historical elements include:

  • The Lost Ninth Legion: One of history's great mysteries—what really happened to Hispana?
  • Emperor Hadrian: The soldier-emperor who consolidated Rome's frontiers
  • Hadrian's Wall: The iconic frontier, its construction and purpose
  • Romano-British Society: The complex interactions between Roman and native cultures
  • Northern Tribes: The Brigantes, Selgovae, and other peoples beyond the frontier
