Primary Sources & Bibliography

The foundation of historical authenticity

Home / Research / Sources

Historical fiction lives or dies by its foundation in truth. These are the primary sources, scholarly works, and archival materials that have shaped my understanding of each era. From ancient chronicles to modern academic studies, each source has contributed to the authenticity of my novels.

Primary Sources Secondary Sources Archives & Collections Complete Bibliography

Primary Sources

Original documents and contemporary accounts from each era

Anglo-Saxon Chronicle

Viking Age, c. 890-1154

A collection of annals in Old English, chronicling the history of the Anglo-Saxons. The original manuscript was commissioned by Alfred the Great and continued after his death.

Old English British Library
View Online Cite

Egil's Saga

Viking Age, c. 13th century recording

An Icelandic saga depicting the life of Egil Skallagrímsson, a Viking warrior and poet. Provides insight into Viking society, warfare, and culture.

Old Norse Árni Magnússon Institute
View Online Cite

Asser's Life of King Alfred

Viking Age, 893 AD

A biography of Alfred the Great written by Asser, a Welsh monk who served at Alfred's court. The only contemporary account of an Anglo-Saxon king.

Latin Cotton MS
View Online Cite

State Papers of Henry VIII

Tudor Era, 1509-1547

Official government documents, letters, and records from the reign of Henry VIII. Includes correspondence with ambassadors, courtiers, and foreign powers.

English, Latin, French The National Archives
View Online Cite

Letters and Papers, Foreign and Domestic

Tudor Era, 16th century

A comprehensive collection of documents from the reign of Henry VIII, compiled in the 19th century. Includes diplomatic correspondence, court records, and personal letters.

English, Latin, Spanish British History Online
View Online Cite

Calendar of State Papers, Spanish

Tudor Era, 1485-1558

Spanish diplomatic correspondence relating to England, providing an external perspective on Tudor court politics and international relations.

Spanish, English British History Online
View Online Cite

Tacitus: Agricola

Roman Britain, 98 AD

A biography of Tacitus' father-in-law, the general Agricola, who served as governor of Britain. The primary literary source for Roman Britain in the 1st century.

Latin Various manuscripts
View Online Cite

Vindolanda Tablets

Roman Britain, c. 90-120 AD

Wooden writing tablets from the Roman fort of Vindolanda near Hadrian's Wall. Include personal letters, military reports, and administrative documents offering intimate glimpses of life on the frontier.

Latin British Museum
View Online Cite

Antonine Itinerary

Roman Britain, 3rd century AD

A register of Roman roads and stations across the empire. For Britain, it details the major routes, distances between settlements, and military installations.

Latin Various manuscripts
View Online Cite
