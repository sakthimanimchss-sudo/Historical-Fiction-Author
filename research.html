The Scholar's Journey

Every novel begins with a question. What was it really like to stand on Hadrian's Wall as a Roman legionary? How did it feel to walk the corridors of the Tudor court? My research is an attempt to answer these questions—to uncover not just the facts, but the lived experience of history.

I believe that authenticity matters. Not just in dates and events, but in the small details: how people spoke, what they ate, how they loved and grieved. I spend months, sometimes years, immersed in primary sources, visiting locations, and consulting with historians before writing a single word.

This section of my website is an invitation to join me on that journey. Explore the real figures, timelines, maps, and sources that bring my novels to life.

Research by Series

The Saxon Chronicles: Research Highlights

For this series, I spent three months in York and Northumbria, working with archaeologists at Jorvik and walking the routes of the Viking armies. Key research areas included:

  • Anglo-Saxon burial practices and grave goods
  • Viking ship construction and navigation
  • The Great Heathen Army's campaign routes
  • Alfred's defensive strategies and burh system
  • Old English language and naming conventions
Visit Series Page

The Tudor Intrigue: Research Highlights

Research for this series took me to the National Archives at Kew, Hampton Court Palace, and the Tower of London. Key areas of study included:

  • Tudor court hierarchy and daily life
  • The Reformation's political and social impact
  • Anne Boleyn's letters and trial records
  • 16th century fashion, food, and medicine
  • The role of women in Tudor society
Visit Series Page

The Roman Eagle: Research Highlights

For this series, I walked Hadrian's Wall, visited Vindolanda, and studied Roman military records. Key research areas included:

  • Roman legion organization and daily life
  • The mystery of the Ninth Legion
  • Hadrian's Wall construction and purpose
  • Roman-British relations and trade
  • Vindolanda tablets and personal letters

My Research Process

01

Primary Source Immersion

I begin with original documents—chronicles, letters, legal records—to understand the period from the inside out.

02

Site Visits & Archaeology

Walking the ground where history happened is essential. I visit battlefields, ruins, and museums whenever possible.

03

Expert Consultation

I work with historians, archaeologists, and specialists to verify details and uncover new perspectives.

04

Material Culture

Understanding how people lived—what they wore, ate, and used—brings authenticity to every scene.
